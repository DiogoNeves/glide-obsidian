# Glide Deep Research Subject

## Load

- `Agent HQ/AGENTS.md`
- `Agent HQ/Research/AGENTS.md`
- `Agent HQ/Research/Research Index.md`
- `Agent HQ/User Profile.md` when the research is meant to inform the user's advice, decisions, goals, or operating system
- Relevant goal, area, project, or decision files

## Process

1. Define the research question and the decision, goal, or area it serves.
2. Use parallel subagents when the topic is broad, uncertain, source-heavy, or benefits from independent perspectives.
3. Search lived-experience sources for patterns, vocabulary, edge cases, and failure modes.
4. Search expert-practice sources: credible practitioners, official guidance, interviews, books, and domain experts.
5. Search empirical sources: papers, systematic reviews, primary data, and reputable institutions.
6. Integrate subagent findings yourself. Resolve conflicts, assess evidence quality, and avoid copying parallel outputs uncritically.
7. Separate observations by evidence type:
   - lived experience
   - expert practice
   - empirical evidence
   - primary documentation
   - speculation or weak signal
8. Look for disagreement and high-quality minority views.
9. Recommend Agent HQ updates when useful.
10. Update `Agent HQ/Research/Research Index.md`.
11. Add a central research note or area research note when the result is durable.

## Output

- Conversational synthesis.
- Evidence map with source links.
- Confidence level and major uncertainties.
- Suggested Agent HQ updates.
- Research note or index update.
